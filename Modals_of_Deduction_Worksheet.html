📚 Grammar: Modals of Deduction

What are Modals of Deduction?

We use modals of deduction to make guesses or conclusions about situations based on evidence or logic.

PRESENT DEDUCTION

Modal + base verb (for present situations)

MUST 95% certain
= I'm almost sure this is true (очень уверен)
She must be tired. She's been working for 12 hours.
Она, должно быть, устала. Она работает уже 12 часов.
MIGHT / MAY / COULD 50% certain
= Perhaps this is true, I'm not sure (возможно)
He might be at home. I'm not sure.
Он, возможно, дома. Я не уверена.
It could be true, but I doubt it.
Это может быть правдой, но я сомневаюсь.
CAN'T 0% certain
= I'm sure this is NOT true (уверен, что это не так)
That can't be John. He's in Paris right now.
Это не может быть Джон. Он сейчас в Париже.
PAST DEDUCTION

Modal + have + past participle (for past situations)

MUST HAVE 95% certain
= I'm almost sure this happened (очень уверен, что это произошло)
She must have forgotten about the meeting.
Она, должно быть, забыла о встрече.
MIGHT HAVE / MAY HAVE / COULD HAVE 50% certain
= Perhaps this happened (возможно, это произошло)
He might have missed the bus.
Он, возможно, опоздал на автобус.
They could have gone to the cinema.
Они могли пойти в кино.
CAN'T HAVE 0% certain
= I'm sure this did NOT happen (уверен, что этого не было)
She can't have said that. She's too polite.
Она не могла этого сказать. Она слишком вежливая.

📊 Certainty Scale:

100% He IS at home. (I know for sure)
95% He MUST BE at home. (almost certain)
50% He MIGHT/MAY/COULD BE at home. (possible)
5% He CAN'T BE at home. (almost impossible)
0% He ISN'T at home. (I know for sure)
